The History and the Uniqueness of the School

  • The pioneer of the school, Late Mr.Vilmot A. Perera believed the fact that aesthetic element must play a vital role in the education of the child.
  • It is with this vision he established this unique school as a very rich and wealthy planter in the area.
  • In setting the stepping the school for this dream to be a reality the challenge of entering into Shanthi Nikethen in India had to be fulfilled and with much effort in 1932,he finally flourished not only in getting the education as a disciple of Gurudave, but one of the most charismatic students of Gurudave Rabeendranath Tagore.
  • On the 20th of May in 1934 he was fortunate to accomplish his dream by laying the foundation stone for the school in the hands of Gurudave Rabeendranath Tagore, the first Nobel Prize winner in Asia.
  • The name ‘Sripalee’ too was given by Gurudewa, with the meaning that it is the place where the Goddess for Art presides. On this ground of the place, in the real sense of the world is a very prestigious and tranquil premise.
  • Within a short period of the time the fame of this school inclined and started reverberating in the hearts and minds of the people in every nook and corner of the island. 
  • As a consequence a large number of students from various directions of the country enter to this school with the intension of receiving a balance diet of education consisted with knowledge, skills and attitudes. 
  • The pioneer, Mr Vilmot A Perera very far slightly to all measures to supply accommodation by providing hostel facilities for all these students from far corners of the country.
  • The students who got the best education in both co – curricular and extra – curricular were truly given the top most priority in climbing up in the ladder of education.
  • This was immensely supported and guided by the principals and teachers who are in thousands in number.
  • As far as the true identity of the school is concerned, the brotherly - sisterly relationship the students maintain is of course unique. Rather than the personal name, each student is addressed either as a brother or sister with a tremendous sense of love, affection and sincerity. This heart - rendering address is commonly seen even among teachers which is also  a unique feature of the school. This has really created a warm and strong bond extending all other ties as these terms addressed to their deep hearts core.
  • Another characteristic feature of this school to a higher rank is the maintenance of the one minute silence which symbolically highlights the unflagging gratitude the students as well as the for the founder , late Mr:Vilmot A. Perera.
  • The silence they again maintain both at the beginning and at the end of the school work also epitomizes the unique resemblance of the school.
  • The Upasanaya , the meeting of the student and teachers is started with the religious observance, is yet another most significant and eye – catching sight one can see in this school premise. The scene of the students after their Upasanaya , they very rhythmically step along the main road of the school under the shade of the huge Kadambarie trees feeling the fragrance of its flowers while singing the Upasana song taking hand in hand is incomparable to any other general meeting of the school in the island.
